~BENTHOTA TURTLE HATCHERY~


Sri Lankans established the Turtle Hatchery to save turtles from extinction. Before, fishermen would harvest turtle eggs and sell them to the highest bidder. With the start of the Turtle Hatchery, these eggs were fed and cared for until they were 2 to 4 years old. There are several turtle species to be seen, including the Green Turtle, Leatherback, and Olive Ridley. Despite their demise, turtles are still caught by fisherman for fish and shells, which is a very lucrative business.The Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project is a famous site in Bentota for all visitors. The gorgeous golden sands that entice you to our island also provide an alluring breeding habitat for numerous endangered turtle species, ranging from the Olive Ridley to the Leatherback turtle. The effort tries to conserve and safeguard the eggs from predators and other threats. Visit the turtle hatchery in Bentota to learn more about these endangered, elegant sea creatures.

Once at the hatchery, the eggs are kept safely buried in sand until they emerge from their shells, when they are moved and placed into tanks for the first few days of their lives. This gives them to opportunity to grow stronger before they are released back to the dangers of the sea, increasing their odds of survival. Several species of turtles are saved in this way.After sunset, you may see the newborn turtles being released on the beach and watching them make their way back to the ocean. Visitors are advised to be conscious of the process of returning newborn turtles to the sea. Loud sounds, bright lights, and flash photography are not permitted; the newborn turtles may mistake the lights for the moon over the ocean, preventing them from returning home.


Destination Information


  • Location: 10 to 15 minute tuk tuk trip south of Bentota
  • Highlights include a visit to the Kasgoda Turtle Hatchery, where you may spend time observing the newborn turtles grow.
  • Points to remember: Avoid shining light on the nesting turtles, touch only if said by authorities.
  • Price: The approximate pricing starts at 500 LKR.
