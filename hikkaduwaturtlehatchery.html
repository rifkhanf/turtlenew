~HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY~


The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre is located a few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town, with the goal of protecting some of the world's most endangered turtle species. It is one of several turtle hatcheries on the island's southern coast. It began as a community project to help maintain the biodiversity of the southern coast and has since grown into a full-fledged hatchery and rescue center with dedicated staff working around the clock to study, care for, and protect many turtle species.The hatchery and rescue center currently cares for five kinds of turtles. Olive Ridley Turtle, Leatherback Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Green Turtle are examples of such species. The sea turtle hatchery and rescue center's facilities are state-of-the-art, with water tanks to care for newborn turtles as well as adult turtle species rescued from the sea due to injuries. One of the more unusual sights in the center is the area where the turtles lay their eggs, which has distinct small mounds of sand piled neatly between one another. Each of these mounds is continuously watched in order to compute the hatching process of the turtle eggs.





Things To Do In Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery


Hikkaduwa is a seaside tourist town in southwestern Sri Lanka. It's famous for its high surf and beaches, notably palm-dotted Hikkaduwa Beach, which is lined with restaurants and bars. The shallow seas opposite Hikkaduwa Beach protect the Hikkaduwa National Park, which is a coral sanctuary and home to marine turtles and unusual fish. The Hikkaduwa turtle hatchery has been a special place for tourists to witness the turtles, which are rare to be seen in other parts of the world.These are some things to do in the relevant hatchery.

1.Hold the turtles.
2.Feed the turtles according to the relevant authority rules and times
3.Sea Turtle Boat Ride- Finding for exotic turtles and fish in the middle of the sea.
4.Visit one of the oldest turtles in the turtle hatchery.
5.Witness egg hatching on summer end.
6.Witness the process of egg hatching conducted by authorities.
